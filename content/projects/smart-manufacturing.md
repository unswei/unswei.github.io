This joint project with the **University of Sydney** and **Western Sydney University** explores how high-performance composite structures can be manufactured more reliably and at lower cost.

The wider project is developing digital twin methods for composite manufacturing processes, with the aim of improving quality, reducing defects, and supporting better real-time decision-making during production.

Our lab’s role focuses on machine learning for physical systems. We are developing models that combine physical knowledge with modern learning methods so they can make fast predictions while still respecting the underlying process physics.

A key part of this work is uncertainty. In manufacturing, it is not enough to predict what is likely to happen. We also want to know how confident the model is about its predictions. We are therefore developing uncertainty-aware physics-informed neural operator models, including new work combining methods to quantify uncertainty with Physics-Informed Neural Operators, to support more trustworthy prediction in tightly toleranced manufacturing settings.
